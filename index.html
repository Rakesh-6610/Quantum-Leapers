Air Pollution

19 th May, 2024

Air pollution is caused by burning fossil fuels, industrial emissions, and agriculture. Key pollutants include sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, carbon monoxide, and particulates. These pollutants harm health, cause acid rain, and contribute to climate change. Prevention includes stricter emission regulations, using renewable energy, improving public transport, and sustainable farming. Catalytic converters and tree planting can also help reduce pollution and its impacts.

DNA: The Blueprint of Life

20 th August, 2024

DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is the molecule that carries the genetic instructions for life. Found in the nucleus of cells, it looks like a twisted ladder, known as a double helix. DNA is made up of four bases: adenine (A), thymine (T), cytosine (C), and guanine (G). These bases pair up (A with T, C with G) to form the rungs of the ladder. DNA determines traits by coding for proteins, which perform various functions in the body. It is passed from parents to offspring, ensuring the continuity of genetic information across generations.

Benefits of Regular Exercise and Hygiene

10 th March, 2024

Regular exercise and good hygiene are essential for a healthy life. Exercise strengthens muscles, bones, and the heart, helps maintain a healthy weight, and reduces stress and anxiety. Good hygiene practices, such as regular handwashing and dental care, prevent infections, illnesses, and dental problems. Together, they boost the immune system, lower the risk of diseases, improve overall well-being, and can increase life expectancy.
